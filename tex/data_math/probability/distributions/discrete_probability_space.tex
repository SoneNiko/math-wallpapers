A discrete probability space is a pair $(\Omega, \mathbb{P})$, where
\begin{align*}
    \text{(1.)} & \hspace{0.5cm} \text{$\Omega$ is a finite or countable set.} \\
    \text{(2.)} & \hspace{0.5cm} \text{$\mathbb{P} : \Omega \to [0,1]$ satisfies $\sum_{\omega \in \Omega} \mathbb{P}\,(\omega) = 1$.} \\
    \text{(3.)} & \hspace{0.5cm} \text{Events are subsets $A \subseteq \Omega$, with $\mathbb{P}\,(A) = \sum_{\omega \in A} \mathbb{P}\,(\omega)$.}
\end{align*}
